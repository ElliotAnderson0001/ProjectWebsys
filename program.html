  1. Chest/Bicep

      2. Chest (Push Movements)

      Flat Barbell Bench Press (Chest, triceps, front delts) 4 sets of 6–8 reps
      Tip: Use a full range of motion, lower the bar to your chest, and keep your feet planted firmly on the ground. Incline Dumbbell Press (Upper chest emphasis)
      4 sets of 8–10 reps
      Tip: Keep a slight arch in your back and control the dumbbells during the descent. Chest Dips (Lower chest focus)
      3 sets of 10–12 reps
      Tip: Lean slightly forward to emphasize the chest. Add weight using a dip belt for progression. Cable Flyes (or Pec Deck) (Chest isolation)
      4 sets of 12–15 reps
      Tip: Perform slow and controlled reps. Squeeze your chest at the peak of each rep. Push-Ups (Burnout Set)
      1 set to failure
      Tip: Perform elevated push-ups or weighted push-ups if bodyweight is too easy.

      Biceps (Pull Movements) Barbell Bicep Curls (Mass builder)

      4 sets of 8–10 reps
      Tip: Keep your elbows stationary and avoid swinging the bar for momentum. Incline Dumbbell Curls (Biceps isolation)
      4 sets of 10–12 reps
      Tip: Use a slight incline (30–45°). Fully stretch your biceps at the bottom of each rep. Concentration Curls (Biceps peak)
      3 sets of 12–15 reps
      Tip: Rest your elbow on your inner thigh for stability. Focus on strict form. Hammer Curls (Brachialis and forearms)
      3 sets of 10–12 reps
      Tip: Keep your palms facing each other throughout the movement. Cable Rope Curls (or Resistance Band Curls)
      3 sets of 15–20 reps (finisher)
      Tip: Use slow, controlled reps to maximize the pump.

  2. Back/Tricep

      3. Back (Pull Movements)

      Pull-Ups (Wide Grip) 4 sets of 6–10 reps
      Progressions: Beginner: Assisted pull-ups (using bands or a machine). Advanced: Weighted pull-ups. Tip: Engage your lats by pulling your shoulder blades down and back. Bodyweight Rows (Australian Rows)
      4 sets of 10–12 reps
      Equipment: Use a low bar or rings. Tip: Keep your body in a straight line, and squeeze your shoulder blades at the top. Superman Holds (Lower Back Isolation)
      3 sets of 20–30 seconds
      Tip: Focus on extending your arms and legs as far as possible while lifting your chest. Archer Pull-Ups (For Advanced Trainees)
      3 sets of 4–8 reps per side
      Progression: Perform standard pull-ups until you're ready. Dead Hang (Grip and Lat Stretch)
      2 sets of 30–60 seconds
      Tip: Hang from a bar with arms fully extended, and allow your shoulders to relax.

      Triceps (Push Movements)

      4 sets of 8–12 reps
      Equipment: Use parallel bars or a sturdy surface. Progressions: Add weight or elevate your feet. Tip: Lean slightly forward to avoid shoulder strain. Pseudo Planche Push-Ups
      3 sets of 8–12 reps
      Progression: Start with regular push-ups, then gradually move your hands closer to your hips. Diamond Push-Ups (Close-Grip Push-Ups)
      3 sets of 10–15 reps
      Tip: Form a diamond shape with your hands and keep your elbows tucked in. Bench Dips (Triceps Isolation)
      3 sets of 10–12 reps
      Tip: Keep your back close to the bench and lower until your arms are at a 90° angle. Resistance Band Overhead Tricep Extensions
      3 sets of 15–20 reps
      Tip: Use a resistance band anchored above your head, and focus on a slow and controlled motion.

  4. Glutes & Legs (Lower Body Focus) Barbell Back Squats (Targets quads, glutes, hamstrings)

    4 sets of 8–10 reps
    Tip: Focus on depth to fully activate the glutes. Romanian Deadlifts (Hamstrings, glutes)
    4 sets of 8–12 reps
    Tip: Keep a slight bend in your knees and hinge at the hips. Hip Thrusts (Glutes isolation)
    4 sets of 10–12 reps
    Tip: Hold the top position for 2 seconds for maximum glute engagement. Bulgarian Split Squats (Glutes, quads, stability)
    3 sets of 10 reps per leg
    Tip: Add dumbbells for intensity. Calf Raises (Calves)
    4 sets of 15–20 reps
    Tip: Perform slow and controlled movements.
